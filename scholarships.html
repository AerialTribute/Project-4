$1,000 Aviation Training Scholarship

Supporting pilots who aspire to serve their communities through humanitarian aviation.

Eligibility

  • Be a U.S. citizen or permanent resident
  • Eligible for an FAA student pilot certificate
  • Enrolled in Part 141 or Part 61 flight training
  • Minimum 10 flight hours

Q1. What can the scholarship be used for?

This award covers the CFI cost and lowers your total cost per lesson.

Q2. How will my award payout?

You login to our website and upload a copy of your receipt and logbook. We will review your documents and reimburse you the CFI cost of your lesson.

Q3. Who can apply to this scholarship program?

We encourage flight training schools to treat their students fairly and have a partnership with many programs spanning from Maine to North Carolina. Click the "Connected Flight Schools" button to see if your program is included.

Q4. How can I get my school into this program?

Email us your school’s contact and we’ll follow up to begin screening. No payment or marketing is required, we simply want an established relationship to keep the pulse on their reputation and culture.

Selection Criteria

Personal Impact and Leadership (1–5 Points)

  • 1 Point: Minimal or no evidence of personal impact or leadership.
  • 2 Points: Some involvement in local flight program demonstrating impact or leadership but lacks depth or significant influence.
  • 3 Points: Adequate demonstration of impact and leadership, with clear examples.
  • 4 Points: Strong leadership and significant impact helping other pilots.
  • 5 Points: Exceptional leadership and transformative impact on the pilot community.

Community Engagement and Service (1–5 Points)

  • 1 Point: Minimal involvement in community activities; participation is sporadic or superficial with little to no impact.
  • 2 Points: Some involvement in community service or projects, but impact remains limited.
  • 3 Points: Active participation in community service or projects with a good level of impact.
  • 4 Points: Strong, sustained involvement with significant impact; demonstrates leadership.
  • 5 Points: Extensive involvement with profound impact; recognized as a community leader.

Resilience and Perseverance (1–5 Points)

  • 1 Point: Shows minimal resilience and struggles significantly with challenges.
  • 2 Points: Displays some resilience; overcomes everyday challenges but falters under stress.
  • 3 Points: Good level of resilience by overcoming notable obstacles.
  • 4 Points: Strong perseverance, overcoming major obstacles for growth.
  • 5 Points: Exceptional resilience; overcame severe setbacks with increased commitment.
